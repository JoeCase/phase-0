Week 1: Version Control

There's been a lot to cover in the past couple of weeks... computer systems setup, introductions, Google Hangouts, and now we're diving a little into version control and a bit of code! So many interesting topics to cover but I've been doing my best try not to go on too long about each aspect of the git commands and all the functions on the command line in my reflections. Overall, it's been a lot to take in - at least for someone coming from a non-tech background. I'm currently working in the translation industry and though I'm passionate about foreign languages and love my job, after trying out programming I've just been hooked and so intrigued by what you can create once you develp a foundation.

​

As we're just a week or so in, I'm clearly still developing this foundation, but it's been quite interesting what I've learned so far! There's been walk-through videos, hands-on practice, and a great community to reach out to if there's ever any questions. Although I've gone over some of these commands before, most of the material is new, so it takes a bit of time before fully understanding the concepet. Luckily, there's been plenty of readings to cover and explain the material in more than one way, and after a bit of practice during the assignments it's starting to all sink in.
